Projects

Use of Untreated Sea Sand as Partial Replacement of River Sand in Concrete

Final Year B.E. Project · Civil & Structural Engineering

This academic project focused on evaluating the feasibility of using untreated sea sand as a partial replacement for conventional river sand (fine aggregate) in concrete. The study aimed to address material scarcity and sustainability concerns associated with excessive river sand extraction.

The work involved literature review, understanding material properties, basic mix proportioning, and analysis of concrete performance characteristics. Emphasis was placed on assessing strength behavior and practical applicability in construction while considering environmental impact.

The project enhanced understanding of concrete materials, aggregate behavior, and sustainable construction practices, and strengthened analytical and documentation skills relevant to civil engineering applications.
